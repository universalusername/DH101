## Reflection
The standalone still images elicit far less emotion compared to when they are stitched together. The neverending loop creates an unnerving sense of unease, the feeling of ‘being watched’ when you least expect it, 24/7, over and over. In today’s society where data reigns king and tracking systems are abundant, this is not a farfetched idea. This is amplified by a fear factor, as GIFs like this one are usually viewed on devices with front facing cameras. Do you trust companies and technology when they say it isn’t on? The GIF makes you second-guess your sense of security and anonymity in the cyber space. Repetition reinforces the neverending cycle of surveillance, suggesting that the eyes are never truly “off…” rather, they are fooling you into thinking so. AI honestly didn’t have much of a hand in this; I was having too much fun using a new tool (CapCut) and making drawings on Google Docs. It did suggest initial designs from which I took inspiration from (and used one, though heavily modified), but the real making happened in the video editing, which I did myself. Overall, this GIF helped me understand the power of ‘pictures’ over ‘words’ (a picture paints 1000 words); Being told you are being watched is nothing compared to the feeling of seeing the eyes themselves. This loop, this pattern reinforces the idea much more than a page filled with the same sentence over and over. AI in this case only created a suggestion, from which I chose to loop, fade, color, layer, and edit into something that holds much more meaning.
