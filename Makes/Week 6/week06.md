# Week 6 – Text & Distant Reading

## The Artifact

Text analyzed: Becoming Eden [It's me I'm trees] by Lenipez

Voyant Analysis
The highest frequency words in the story are right, hand, time, don’t, and know. Trend lines showed large groupings in frequency jumps with words relating to the body (hand, mind, eyes, face, arm), particularly ¾ of the way through the story. The links showed strong connections between terms (don’t and know; I’m and sorry) but couldn’t show the meaning of nor explain the reasons and ideas behind those connections. Voyant helped me make connections between the main character’s mental state and physical transformation (linked frequency pattern similarities between mind and body words), but failed to explain the importance and meaning behind these patterns and connections.

GPT Analysis
Prompt: (Uploaded the PDF) “Literary analysis;; 5 paragraphs or less. Focus on themes of dual metabolic states and singularity vs. multiplicity.”
ChatGPT was able to parse through the heavy poetic and synesthesic formatting to explain the surface level connections to the Catholic faith as well as the process of transcendence and its effects on identity. It highlighted themes such as “trauma, survival, and agency”, and “inevitability vs. choice”. The analysis was surprisingly vague and when asked for more explanation, GPT repeated previous statements, seemingly unable to cite specific examples in the text and unable to elaborate.

Comparison
Voyant analyzes words as data, recognizing repetition and words that are commonly connected, HOWEVER, it provides no analysis of themes or deeper meaning. GPT analyzes themes as a real person might, making connections to greater ideas based on the entire story. HOWEVER, it fails to elaborate on the how and why it came to those conclusions, providing little context and evidence for its bold claims. Voyant displays objective facts about the nature of the composition of the story by showing connections, repetitions, etc., but is unable to make further analysis. GPT provides that further analysis, but lacks the reliable data structure that Voyant does, making every claim up for debate no matter how solidly it presents its ‘findings.’

## Reflection
Voyant recognizes patterns in word repetition and connected words in sentence structure. Though it displays this data in a way that might allow a human reader to assign meaning to the highlighted patterns, it ultimately misses things such as implied themes (such as the fact that the story I chose is an allegory of the transgender experience, among other things) and infrequent words that might have central meaning. The emphasis on high frequency = most meaning can be true in some contexts, but in others it may prove less helpful and even distract a reader from the true meaning of a text.
GPT fills in the missing ‘meaning’ parts of the text analysis. It crafts an easily comprehensible summary of main points, themes, and symbolism in the text. Every conclusion it presents is done with an unwavering tone that brokers no negotiation. That ‘confidence’ makes GPT seem reliable and knowledgeable about the text when in fact it is comparing the patterns it ‘reads’ to other works, not specifically the one intended. Prompting for analysis on a paragraph or sentence level of a text knocks its certainty down a few notches and can even contradict the conclusions it came to in the full text analysis.
Both of these tools simply recognize patterns rather than ‘read’ as you or I might. While these tools can analyze large amounts of text in a short amount of time, increasing speed/efficiency, they fail to consider personal, emotional, time period, technology, and other factors that impact how humans view the meaning of a text. While these machines are useful for expanding upon our own conclusions, what they state should not be relied upon as written in stone or absolute fact. Humans reading and sharing their own conclusions about a text highlights the nuance in our individual experience; how one’s ethnicity, gender, level of education, belief system, and other human context shape and change how we assign meaning to text. Voyant and GPT can assist in this process, but should never overtake our ability to interpret meaning ourselves and our create personal definitions of authenticity.
