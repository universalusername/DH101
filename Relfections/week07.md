# Week 7 Reflection

Reflect 7: How does AI reshape global geographies of power, labor, and environment?

I would argue that AI isn't reshaping anything, rather, it is perpetuating old historical patterns of exploitation and benefits at the cost of human suffering. Data centers are built primarily in the global north, which serves the consumer base that, you guessed it, lives in the global north. Subsequently, lesser developed countries are being exploited for cheap labor and resource extraction, often with little regard to safety and the environment. Communities are uprooted, workers lose their lives in dangerous working conditions, costs are cut by taking advantage of human trafficking networks and child labor, and acres upon acres of forests, deserts, and salt flats are contaminated beyond repair. Data centers are no saints either; their air pollution has caused upticks in lung conditions, their water usage threatens to create water scarcity issues, and their noise and energy requirements drive out locals. In the end, AI infrastructure keeps the power in the hands of the company, (not the workers) and benefits little compared to its harm. Cycles of inequality continue on, as the north continues its neocolonial rule over the south.
