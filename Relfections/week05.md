# Week 5 Reflection

Reflect 5: How does AI alter authorship and remix culture? Who owns AI-made art?

AI changes ownership by its very nature; as a tool built upon the works of others, it intrinsically creates shared content. Ownership of AI art is muddled by the fact that the owners of the content used to train the machine are not properly accredited, which is similar to writing a research paper that doesn't cite any sources. Despite this, it is undeniable that AI art is a group process: original work --> AI training --> human prompt --> AI generation. It creates a collaborative system (even if some parts did not consent) that distributes ownership between the original sources' authors, the company that coded the AI, and the person prompting and changing the generated content. This is to say that all AI generated contents are remixed works that take patterns found across its training data to create something relatively new. The question is this, however: how much effort and change does it take for the prompter to be considered on the same level as a beginner artist? After all, a beginner will take inspiration and patterns (artstyles) from existing works in order to practice and get better at their own works, much like AI.
