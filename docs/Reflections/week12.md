# Ecology

## Is AI sustainable? What ecological trade-offs are we willing to accept?

As it currently stands, AI is not sustainable. Much like every other consumer-oriented product/service, it draws upon dwindling resources and exploits vulnerable populations in order to function. From its beginnings as rare earth minerals and metals to its end in a landfill, AI uses immense amounts of energy, water, and human labor. Often, these sources are nonrenewable and unethical.

For a better (but realistic) future, society must choose between speed, convenience, the warming of the earth, the shrinking of the coastlines, and the loss of its forests. These trade-offs can be offset by serious investments in carbon sinks, renewable energy sources, and a willingness to sacrifice convenience for a cleaner system and healthier planet. There is no going back now that we have invented/discovered AI, so the best course of action is to make it have the smallest impact that we can.
