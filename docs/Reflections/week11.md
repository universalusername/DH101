# Visibility

## Who does the invisible work of AI, and who profits from it?

The invisible work behind AI is typically done by a vast workforce of outsourced workers across the global south. Data labelers and annotators tag images, text, and audio so AI models can learn patterns, while content moderators review flagged posts to remove material that violates platform policies. These jobs are repetitive, underpaid, and psychologically damaging, and yet they are essential for the existence of AI systems.

Despite performing the foundational work, these workers rarely share in the profits generated. Instead, powerful corporations such as Google, OpenAI, Meta, etc. obtain billions from the fruit of these workers' labor; a prime example of exploitation. Outsourcing companies also profit from these workers, as they are able to sell their cheap labor to those corporations. Overall, the system demonstrates an imbalance: numerous workers suffer to enable AI, but profits are instead concentrated among a small group of powerful corporations.
